`# Training Framework Redesign Proposal (Success Team)

**Prepared by:** AK | Senior Training & Knowledge Base Specialist
**Date:** February 2026

---

## Summary

This proposal recommends restructuring the generic new hire training program (Days 1-2) to reduce content overload, eliminate redundant teaching, and improve knowledge retention - starting with the Success team track (CSM, MOM, OS & OC).

The redesign moves from the current one-size-fits-all approach to a "Foundation → Practice → Fork" model that keeps all roles together for shared fundamentals, then splits into track-specific depth before Day 3.

**Key outcome:** Reduce total Success-track slide consumption from 160 to +-110 slides (31% reduction) while improving the quality of learning through structured guided practice instead of passive self-study.

---

## Current Challenges

### 1. One size fits all content creates irrelevant cognitive load

Every role from BC (who needs pitching skills) to Merchant Care (who need troubleshooting depth) sits through the same 56 slides at the same depth and takes the same 48 question quiz. Sales roles absorb deep product configuration details they'll rarely use, while Success roles don't get enough depth on the features and system logic they'll work with daily.

Recent feedback from new hires reinforces this — the Advanced System content in particular has too many main points without clear prioritisation. New hires report feeling unsure of where to focus, especially when the same topics appear at surface level on Day 1 and then reappear in detail on Day 3 without a clear connection between the two passes.

### 2. Passive self-study isn't creating understanding

The Day 2 morning is currently structured as continued self study, followed by a 4 hour sync up session in the afternoon where the trainer recaps topics, runs the hardware demo, and discusses quiz answers.

The most common confusion point during this sync up is BackOffice functions and logic — the exact topics covered through passive slide reading. This pattern confirms that the current delivery method (read slides & watch videos independently → ask questions later) is not producing the understanding we need.

New hire feedback is consistent on this point: BackOffice should be hands-on and live, not read about independently. Multiple new hires expressed a preference for a blend of study followed by immediate hands-on practice — not extended reading blocks followed by delayed application. As one new hire put it, practical and hands-on elements must be more than just reading.

### 3. The BackOffice practical assignment

We have an excellent hands-on BackOffice assignment that forces real learning through practice. However, 2 blockers reduce its effectiveness:

- New hires often wait 2–3 days for full BackOffice access from the billing team, meaning they can't practice during training.
- The trainer struggles to verify completion one by one in larger cohorts, so it becomes a trust-based exercise rather than a verified learning activity.

### 4. The current structure doesn't accommodate different learning speeds

Some new hires move through content quickly but only at surface level — they catch up in speed but don't capture the context of the product. Others are more hands-on learners who struggle with extended reading-heavy modules. The current structure treats everyone the same regardless of learning pace or style, with no mechanism to identify who has genuinely understood the material versus who has simply finished the slides.

Feedback from recent new hires confirms this gap: some prefer a structured flow of study → hands-on → quiz, while others need more practical elements woven throughout. Both groups agree that the current schedule feels tight and pressured, leaving little room to absorb content at their own pace before being assessed on it.

### 5. Too much reading, not enough doing

The heavy reliance on slide-based self-study creates a passive learning environment that disadvantages practical learners. New hires who learn best by doing — configuring, clicking, troubleshooting — are forced into hours of reading before they get to interact with the actual product. By the time they reach hands-on activities, they've already disengaged from the content.

This is the most consistent piece of new hire feedback: the number of slides and the visual density feel overwhelming and heavy. Reporting slides in particular were flagged as text-heavy and difficult to follow. New hires consistently asked for more visual content over reading, and suggested the assessment format itself could be more engaging — such as gamified elements rather than standard Google Form quizzes. The preference is clear: a mixture of practical and interactive learning, not extended passive consumption.

## The Proposed Solution: "Foundation → Practice → Fork"

### Design Principles

Before detailing the structure, here are the principles guiding this redesign:

- **Design for the Learning Hub.** We now have a Learning Hub MVP, and one of the goals of this revamp is to structure training content so it complements and extends the hub. This means designing modules that can live as interactive, self-paced content on the Learning Hub — not just slides to be read in a room. The restructured content should allow hubbers to depend on the Learning Hub to progress in their learning independently, enabling us to evolve from purely trainer-led delivery toward a blended model over time.
- **Teach it once, teach it right.** No topic should be introduced at surface level and then retaught from scratch later. If we introduce a feature on Day 1, Day 3 should deepen it, not restart it.
- **Active learning over passive reading.** Where possible, replace "read these slides" with guided practice and live walkthroughs.
- **Role relevant depth at the right time.** Shared foundation for what everyone needs, then track specific content for what each cluster actually uses in their role.
- **Preserve what works.** The Day 3+ role specific training structure and coach assignments remain unchanged. This redesign targets Days 1-2 only.

### The New Structure

```
DAY 1: FOUNDATION (All Roles Together)
├── 1:00–2:00pm  │ Training Briefing (1hr)
├── 2:00–4:00pm  │ Company + Product Overview (~20 slides)
├── 4:00–6:30pm  │ Core Features + System Intro (~15 slides)
│                │ → Total: ~35 slides (down from 56)
└── Homework     │ Explore BackOffice (access permitting)

DAY 2: PRACTICE + FORK
├── 9:30–11:30am │ Guided BackOffice Walkthrough — LIVE
│                │ → Trainer walks through while trainees follow along
│                │ → Covers System Navigation + Merchant Profile with context
│                │ → Replaces passive self-study with structured guided practice
├── 11:30–1:00pm │ BackOffice Practice Assignment (hands-on, in class)
│                │ → Supervised with spot-check verification
├── 1:00–2:00pm  │ Lunch
├── 2:00–3:00pm  │ Hardware Demo (all roles, live)
├── 3:00–4:00pm  │ Foundation Quiz — 25 questions (trimmed, focused)
├── 4:00–4:30pm  │ Debrief + Fork Briefing
│
│    ┌───────────┴───────────┐
│    │                       │
├── 4:30–6:30pm              ├── 4:30–6:30pm
│ SALES TRACK INTRO          │ SUCCESS TRACK INTRO
│ (BC, SC)                   │ (Care, CSM, MOM, OS, OC)
│ "Merchant Matching &       │ "Advanced System Preview"
│  Pitching Prep"            │ Reports intro + Inventory
│ → 8 slides                 │ → 12 slides
│ → Bridge to Day 3 skills   │ → Bridge to Day 3 deep dive
└────────────────────────────┘

DAY 3+: ROLE-SPECIFIC (Current structure, streamlined)
├── Sales roles  → Jump directly into Pitching/SPIN/Closing
└── Success roles → Continue Advanced System from where preview left off
    → Eliminates re-teaching of Promotions, QR, Beep, Membership, Engage
    → Day 3 picks up at Reports Troubleshooting, CSV, Pricebook, FDI, MRS
```

### What Changes (and What Doesn't)

| Element | Current | Proposed |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Day 1 slides | 56 slides, all roles same depth | ~35 slides, trimmed to universal essentials |
| Day 2 morning | Passive self-study (slides) | Live guided BackOffice walkthrough |
| Day 2 afternoon | 4-hour reactive sync-up | Hardware demo + focused quiz + fork briefing |
| Quiz | 48 generic questions | 25 foundation questions (targeted to what was taught) |
| Track split | Doesn't happen until Day 3 | Begins Day 2 afternoon (2-hour bridge session) |
| Day 3 Advanced deck | 104 slides including re-taught content | ~65 slides (redundant topics removed) |
| BackOffice assignment | Floating homework, hard to verify | In-class supervised exercise with spot-checks |
| Day 3+ role-specific training | Current structure | **No change** |

## How This Addresses Each Challenge

| Challenge | How the Redesign Solves It |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| One-size-fits-all content | Day 2 afternoon fork splits Sales and Success into track-relevant depth. Success roles get system preview; Sales roles get merchant matching. Neither sits through irrelevant content. |
| Passive self-study not working | Day 2 morning becomes a live guided BackOffice walkthrough — directly addressing new hire feedback that BackOffice should be hands-on and live. |
| BackOffice assignment blockers | Assignment moves into Day 2 morning as a supervised in-class exercise. Trainer verifies via spot-check circuit instead of one-by-one. Access pre-provisioning to be coordinated with billing team. |
| Different learning speeds | The guided walkthrough allows real-time pacing. The study → hands-on → quiz flow that new hires preferred is built into the Day 2 structure by design. Modular content on the Learning Hub enables self-paced review. |
| Too much reading | Day 1 reduced from 56 to 35 slides with more visual, less text-heavy design. Day 2 replaces reading with doing. Reporting content (flagged as especially overwhelming) restructured with visual-first approach. Future quiz formats can incorporate more engaging, gamified elements. |
