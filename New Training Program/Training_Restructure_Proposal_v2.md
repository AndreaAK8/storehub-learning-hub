# Training Restructure Proposal: "Foundation > Configure > Prove > Troubleshoot"

**Prepared by:** AK | Senior Training & Knowledge Base Specialist
**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Draft for Stakeholder Review

---

## Summary

This proposal restructures the generic new hire training program (Days 1-4) to reduce content overload, eliminate redundant teaching, and improve knowledge retention -- covering **both Sales (BC, SC) and Success (CSM, MOM, OS, OC)** in a single unified track before forking into role-specific depth.

The redesign moves from the current one-size-fits-all approach to a **"Foundation > Configure > Prove > Troubleshoot"** model that keeps all roles together for shared fundamentals (Days 1-2), forks at end of Day 2 (Sales to coach, Success continues), then gives Success a dedicated integrations and troubleshooting day (Day 3) and hardware assessment with coach handoff (Day 4).

**Key outcomes:**
- Reduce total slide consumption by ~31% while improving learning quality
- Replace passive self-study with trainer-led configuration, interleaving demonstration and hands-on practice throughout Day 2
- Merge Sales and Success into shared Days 1-2 (trainer efficiency)
- Structured industry split: F&B vs Retail/Service product configuration as Day 1 homework, freeing Day 2 for features
- Dedicated integration configuration + troubleshooting day (Day 3, Success only) builds real diagnostic instincts
- Clean fork at end of Day 2: quiz proves foundation, then Sales goes to coach while Success continues with trainer

---

## Current Challenges

### 1. One-size-fits-all content creates irrelevant cognitive load

Every role from BC (who needs pitching skills) to Merchant Care (who need troubleshooting depth) sits through the same 56 slides at the same depth and takes the same 48-question quiz. Sales roles absorb deep product configuration details they will rarely use, while Success roles do not get enough depth on the features and system logic they will work with daily.

Recent feedback from new hires reinforces this -- the Advanced System content in particular has too many main points without clear prioritisation. New hires report feeling unsure of where to focus, especially when the same topics appear at surface level on Day 1 and then reappear in detail on Day 3 without a clear connection between the two passes.

### 2. Passive self-study is not creating understanding

The Day 2 morning is currently structured as continued self-study, followed by a 4-hour sync-up session in the afternoon where the trainer recaps topics, runs the hardware demo, and discusses quiz answers.

The most common confusion point during this sync-up is BackOffice functions and logic -- the exact topics covered through passive slide reading. This confirms that the current delivery method (read slides and watch videos independently, then ask questions later) is not producing the understanding we need.

New hire feedback is consistent: BackOffice should be hands-on and live, not read about independently. Multiple new hires expressed a preference for a blend of study followed by immediate hands-on practice -- not extended reading blocks followed by delayed application.

### 3. The BackOffice practical assignment has blockers

We have an excellent hands-on BackOffice assignment that forces real learning through practice. However, two blockers reduce its effectiveness:

- New hires often wait 2-3 days for full BackOffice access from the billing team, meaning they cannot practice during training.
- The trainer struggles to verify completion one-by-one in larger cohorts, so it becomes a trust-based exercise rather than a verified learning activity.

### 4. The current structure does not accommodate different learning speeds

Some new hires move through content quickly but only at surface level. Others are hands-on learners who struggle with extended reading-heavy modules. The current structure treats everyone the same regardless of learning pace or style, with no mechanism to identify who has genuinely understood the material versus who has simply finished the slides.

### 5. Too much reading, not enough doing

The heavy reliance on slide-based self-study creates a passive learning environment. New hires who learn best by doing -- configuring, clicking, troubleshooting -- are forced into hours of reading before they get to interact with the actual product. By the time they reach hands-on activities, they have already disengaged from the content.

This is the most consistent piece of new hire feedback: the number of slides and the visual density feel overwhelming. New hires consistently asked for more visual and practical content, and suggested the assessment format could be more engaging.

---

## Design Principles

- **Design for the Learning Hub.** Structure training content so it complements and extends the Learning Hub. Modules should live as interactive, self-paced content -- enabling a blended model over time.
- **Teach it once, teach it right.** No topic should be introduced at surface level and then retaught from scratch later. If we introduce a feature on Day 1, later days should deepen it, not restart it.
- **Active learning over passive reading.** Replace "read these slides" with guided practice and live walkthroughs wherever possible.
- **Role-relevant depth at the right time.** Shared foundation for what everyone needs, then track-specific content for what each role actually uses daily.
- **80/20 industry split.** BackOffice is one platform. About 80% of configuration is identical across F&B and Retail/Service. Teach the shared core once, then teach the 20% that differs (product setup and industry-specific features) as layers on top.
- **Preserve what works.** Day 5+ role-specific training structure and coach assignments remain unchanged. Day 4 afternoon coach handoff structure is preserved.

| 1:30-2:15 | 45m | Report Discrepancies | "Why don't my numbers match?" -- the single most common merchant question for Success roles. **Closing time:** If closing time is 12 AM, the "day" runs 12:00 AM to 11:59 PM. If closing time is 5 AM (bars/clubs), the "day" runs 5:00 AM to 4:59 AM. A transaction at 2 AM falls in "yesterday" for a 5 AM close but "today" for a 12 AM close. ## The Sales Merge: How It Works

BC (Business Consultant) and SC (Sales Coordinator) join Success roles for Days 1-2. The merge works because:

**Days 1-2 are genuinely relevant to Sales:**
- Day 1: Sales needs product knowledge to sell. The features ecosystem map and merchant profile framework is essentially a sales qualification framework. Homework gives them first-hand configuration experience.
- Day 2: Sales needs to understand what configuration looks like to set expectations with merchants and demo BackOffice on sales calls. The interleaved "demo then configure" format gives them hands-on experience with every feature they will talk about -- Online Platforms, Loyalty & Pricing, Stock, Hardware, POS Navigation. When a BC demos BackOffice to a merchant, they need to know where things are and how they work. The foundation quiz at end of Day 2 verifies shared knowledge.

**The fork is clean:**
- Day 2 end (5:45 PM): Fork briefing -- clear visual of the two tracks diverging.
- Day 3 onwards: Sales is full-day with their Sales coach (pitching, call flow, merchant matching, closing skills). Success is with the trainer for integrations + troubleshooting.
- Day 4: Sales continues with their coach. Success has hardware assessment then coach handoff.
- Day 5+: Both tracks are fully role-specific with their respective coaches.

**For SC specifically (who qualifies leads):**
- The features ecosystem map (Day 1) gives them the vocabulary to qualify accurately ("Does this merchant need KDS? Are they multi-outlet?")
- The hands-on feature configuration (Day 2) gives them enough context to ask the right qualifying questions -- they have actually set up Membership, Promotions, QR Order, and Pricebooks
- The foundation quiz (Day 2) confirms they have the product knowledge to qualify leads accurately

## Blended Delivery: Homework Videos + Trainer-Led Configuration + Hands-On Practice

### The Delivery Model: "Video Homework > Trainer-Led Day > Hands-On Exercises"

The redesign uses three delivery modes across the training week:

**Day 1 Evening -- Video-guided homework.** Trainees configure settings, products, modifiers, and variants following short video guides on the Learning Hub. These are procedural tasks that benefit from pause/rewind/rewatch. Each video walks through the exact steps. The trainer does not need to spend Day 2 on these topics -- they verify completion in 15 minutes at start of Day 2.

**Day 2 -- Trainer-led interleaved configuration.** Each feature block follows a consistent pattern: trainer demos the feature (15-30 min), trainees configure it immediately (15-30 min). This cycle repeats throughout the day. The trainer is active all day, but the interleaved format prevents sustained lecturing fatigue.

**Day 3 -- Trainer-led configuration + scenario exercises (Success only).** Morning: integrations follow the same demo-then-configure pattern. Afternoon: troubleshooting uses scenario exercises and the "Break It and Fix It" hands-on format, where trainees diagnose and fix pre-set problems.

### Why This Model Works

Products and settings as homework (with videos):
- These are procedural: follow the steps, fill in the fields, create the product
- Video guides allow self-pacing -- fast learners finish in 45 min, others take 90 min
- Trainer time is freed for the feature blocks that genuinely need live explanation

Interleaved demo-then-configure on Day 2:
- Each feature block ends with trainees having actually configured it -- not just watched it
- The trainer catches misunderstandings in real-time during the practice portion
- No long passive stretches -- the longest single demo block is the Loyalty & Pricing program (1.5 hrs), which itself alternates between trainer walkthrough and hands-on

Scenario-based troubleshooting on Day 3:
- "Break It and Fix It" stations force active problem-solving, not passive listening
- Real merchant scenarios (report discrepancies, hardware failures) build the diagnostic instincts Success roles need

### Future State: Flipped Classroom

Once videos are established and tested, future cohorts can evolve toward a fully flipped model:
- **Day 2 features as pre-work:** Record the Day 2 feature demos as videos. Trainees watch before Day 2. Day 2 becomes full-day practice + Q&A + advanced scenarios.
- **Trainer role shifts:** From "demo-then-configure" to "full-day circulating coach."
- **Scalability:** With all content on video, cohort size becomes less constrained by trainer bandwidth.

This is not required for the first cohort but becomes possible once the Day 2 demo topics are also recorded as videos.
